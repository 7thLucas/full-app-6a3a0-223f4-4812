## Festa — Design Guidelines

### Color
- **Primary:** Premium teal `#0F766E` — used for primary actions, accents, active states, and brand marks.
- **Surface:** White `#FFFFFF` backgrounds with `slate-50` for subtle panels/cards.
- **Text:** `slate-900` for headings, `slate-600` for body, `slate-400/500` for secondary/captions.
- **Borders:** `slate-200` hairlines for cards and dividers.
- **Availability status colors (clear, semantic):**
  - Available → green (`emerald-500` / `emerald-100` bg).
  - Reserved → amber (`amber-500` / `amber-100` bg).
  - Sold → red/slate muted (`rose-500` or `slate-400`, clearly "unavailable").

### Typography
- Strong editorial typography. Extra-bold, tight-tracked display headings (`tracking-tight`, `leading-[1.05]`) for hero/section titles.
- Clear hierarchy: oversized headlines, comfortable body at relaxed line-height, small uppercase eyebrow labels with wide letter-spacing (`tracking-[0.2em] uppercase`) in teal.

### Layout & spacing
- Generous spacing and whitespace — calm, premium, uncluttered.
- **Mobile-first responsive** that also works cleanly on web. Listing grids collapse gracefully to single column on mobile.
- Rounded cards (`rounded-2xl`), soft hairline borders, restrained elevation (subtle shadows, not heavy).

### Imagery
- Imagery-forward listing cards — high-quality property photography is the hero of each card. Large cover images, object-cover, with content below.
- Subtle teal-tinted gradient overlays on hero imagery for brand cohesion and text legibility.

### Components
- **Listing cards:** big image, title, location, key specs (beds/baths/size), price, availability badge.
- **Unit detail:** gallery, spec grid, floor plan, facilities list, price + payment scheme, prominent inquiry / book-visit / WhatsApp CTAs.
- **Status badges:** pill-shaped, semantic color.
- **CTAs:** filled teal primary buttons; clear, single-focus per screen.
- **Dashboards:** efficient, data-clear, tabular where appropriate; calm not flashy.

### Personality
Professional and calm, trustworthy, premium — not flashy. The product should feel like a high-end real estate brand: confident, editorial, and effortless.
